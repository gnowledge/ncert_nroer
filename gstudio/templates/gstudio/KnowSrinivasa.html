Srinivasa Ramanujan FRS (22nd December, 1887 - 26th April, 1920) was an Indian mathematician and autodidact who, with almost no formal training in pure mathematics, made extraordinary contributions to mathematical analysis, number theory, infinite series, and continued fractions.
